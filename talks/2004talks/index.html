Presentations for 2004

February 2004
Benchmarking, DATA Filehandle, our and my
March 2004
The many options that Perl provides to deal with command line options.
April 2004
April didn't have slides as we discussed a mail conversion project and the tools used to handle problems.
May 2004
May saw us talking about the many builtin modules that come with the standard Perl install. We couldn't cover all of them, but we did cover the most relevant.
June 2004
June got us into statement modifiers and loop control, some of the more confusing regex modifiers and the sometimes frustrating map function.
July 2004
July we started our series on Perl and the Internet. The first item we are covering is TemplateToolkit.
August 2004
August  we got into CGI.pm since we had covered the basics of what you could do with TT2 without using CGI, so we covered enough of CGI.pm to allow continued exploration of TT2 if desired.
September 2004
September G. Wade Johnson gave an excellent talk on Perl/TK. If you missed it, you missed a chance to hear from one of our Guru's sharing his personal knowledge with us.
October 2004
October found G. Wade Johnson giving another great talk on "Perl DBI". If you missed it, shame on you.
November 2004

In November, we had two presentations. Paul Archer made a short presentation on the vim editor.

The other subject selected was "A Perl based webserver" in less than 100 lines of code. Once again, G. Wade Johnson did his usual top-of-the-line professional presentation as you can see in these slides.
