Adding and Removing Elements from an Array

Perl arrays are dynamic, meaning they will grow or shrink as necessary in your program (at least until you run of memory).

The push() and pop() functions insert and remove elements from the end of the array, and the shift() and unshift() functions do the same for the beginning of the array: 

    
    my @array = (1, 2, 3);
    push @array, 42;      # @array now contains: (1, 2, 3, 42)
    push @array, (4, 5);  # @array now contains: (1, 2, 3, 42, 4, 5)

    my $last  = pop(@array);   # removes last element from @array
    my $first = shift(@array); # removes first element from @array

    unshift @array, $last;   # shoves $last onto front of @array
    unshift @array, (13,14); # shoves list onto front of @array

    print "@array\n"; # prints: 13 14 5 2 3 42 4
