5.3 Exact Strings

    Perl (and other systems) use the Boyer-Moore algorithm to speed up matches for exact strings in the expression.
    This works especially well in two situations.
      The exact string is at the beginning of the regex.
      Not capturing, just need to know if the regex matches.
    In both cases, a no match result may be returned quickly.
    If the string is at the beginning, it works sort of like an anchor.
